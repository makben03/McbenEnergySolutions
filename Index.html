Uganda's Oil & Gas Affairs

Uganda is on the brink of becoming a significant oil producer with development of the Tilenga and Kingfisher projects in the Albertine Graben region. First oil is expected by 2025, with production projected to reach 230,000 barrels per day at peak.

The East African Crude Oil Pipeline (EACOP) will transport Uganda's crude oil to the Tanzanian port of Tanga for export. This $3.5 billion project represents one of the largest infrastructure investments in East Africa.

Uganda's petroleum sector is governed by the Petroleum (Exploration, Development and Production) Act and the Petroleum (Refining, Conversion, Transmission and Midstream Storage) Act, which provide a comprehensive legal framework for the industry.

With over 6.5 billion barrels of oil reserves discovered, Uganda is positioning itself as an important player in Africa's energy landscape, with emphasis on local content development and sustainable practices.
